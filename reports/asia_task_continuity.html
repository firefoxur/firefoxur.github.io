Multi-Device Task Continuity in Asia

Date: July 2015

Product: Firefox Desktop & Mobile

Location: United States

Researchers: Gemma Petrie & Bill Selman

Background

Software and hardware choices are increasingly becoming choices between noncompatible brand ecosystems. In today’s marketplace, a consumer’s smartphone choice is likely to have far-reaching influence on options for everything from file storage to wearable technology. With each app purchase or third-party service signup, consumers become further invested in a closed brand ecosystem.

Mozilla is interested in integrating its products and services into the ubiquitous computing framework both of out of strategic necessity and to deliver fully integrated, delightful user experiences across devices. More importantly, Mozilla wants its products and services integrated in a way commensurate with its values of openness and user choice. While also informing future product and service strategy, user research on current and emerging task continuity behavior will help Mozilla design our current products and services to support users’ behaviors and mental models

Methods
  • Eight days of fieldwork in Taipei and New Taipei City, Taiwan and Tokyo and Suburbs, Japans.
  • Recruited a demographically diverse range of 10 participants.
  • All 10 primary participants created a pre-interview map of the devices, services, and technological connections in their life.
  • 13 total participants.
  • 90-minute in-home interviews.
